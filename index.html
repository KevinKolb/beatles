How each bar was scored

Each song starts from a 100-point budget that is split between contributors, then converted to the percentages you see in the bar. The split rewards three things, weighted by how much each one defines a recording:

Writing  45 pts  → split by documented primary authorship
Lead vocal  20 pts
Backing vocal  10 pts  → shared evenly among the backing singers
Playing  25 pts  → split across who actually played on the take
───────────────────────────────
Each person's share % = their points ÷ the song's total points.

Writing: the credit vs. the truth

Almost every original was published as "Lennon-McCartney" and split 50/50 by contract. That is not what this measures. The writing points follow who actually wrote it, drawn from the band's own interviews and standard references. So "Yesterday" and "Blackbird" read as essentially all Paul, "Strawberry Fields Forever" and "Come Together" as all John, and the Harrison and Starr songs sit with their authors. Where authorship is genuinely disputed (e.g. the melody of "In My Life", the lyric of "Eleanor Rigby") the points are split and flagged in the note.

Vocals & playing

Lead vocals are well documented; co-leads and three-part leads (e.g. "Because") are split. Backing-vocal and instrument splits use a documented-exceptions model: a standard four-piece take divides the playing evenly, and named departures override it: Paul on drums for "Back in the U.S.S.R.", Clapton's lead on "While My Guitar Gently Weeps", the all-session strings on "Eleanor Rigby" and "She's Leaving Home", George's Bombay session on "The Inner Light", or just Paul and Ringo on "Why Don't We Do It in the Road?".

The fifth fader: Non-Beatle

A fifth contributor absorbs everything the four didn't do: the writers of covers (so "Twist and Shout" or "Roll Over Beethoven" carry a 45-point Non-Beatle writing block), and session/orchestral players and guests (the quartet on "Yesterday", the orchestra on "A Day in the Life", Billy Preston on "Get Back", Brian Jones on "You Know My Name"). On a normal band original it's zero.

Scope & honesty

  • 213 tracks: the UK studio albums (Please Please Me → Let It Be), Magical Mystery Tour, the new Yellow Submarine songs, and the non-album singles, EP and German recordings (Past Masters).
  • Excluded: BBC and live-only covers, Decca/Anthology outtakes, the orchestral Yellow Submarine film score, and the fan-club Christmas records.
  • The weights, and many of the authorship and backing splits, are an interpretive model, not objective fact, and reasonable fans will argue some of them. Every number is embedded in this page; use the Download data button up top to export it all as a CSV you can open and edit in a spreadsheet.
